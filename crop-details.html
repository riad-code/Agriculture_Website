Featured Crop Profiles

Wheat

Varieties: Durum, Hard Red, Soft Red.
Cultivation: Sown in cool season, needs full sun.
Soil: Well-drained loamy soil.
Harvesting: 100–120 days after sowing.
Nutritional Value: High in carbohydrates, fiber.
Potential Uses: Bread, pasta, cereals.

Mango

Varieties: Alphonso, Himsagar, Langra.
Cultivation: Tropical climate, sown in March-July.
Soil: Fertile, well-drained soil.
Harvesting: 3–6 months after flowering.
Nutritional Value: Rich in Vitamin A, C.
Potential Uses: Juices, pickles, fresh fruit.

Tomato

Varieties: Roma, Cherry, Beefsteak.
Cultivation: Warm-season crop, requires full sunlight.
Soil: Well-drained, slightly acidic soil.
Harvesting: 60–85 days after planting.
Nutritional Value: Rich in Vitamin C, potassium.
Potential Uses: Sauces, salads, soups.

Rice

Varieties: Basmati, Jasmine, Arborio.
Cultivation: Requires flooded fields (paddy), warm climate.
Soil: Clayey or loamy soil with good water retention.
Harvesting: 3–6 months after sowing.
Nutritional Value: High in carbohydrates, small amounts of protein.
Potential Uses: Staple food, rice flour, brewing.
